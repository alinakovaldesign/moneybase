# CONTENT-001 — Consent + card-link explainer copy deck

**Goal**: The exact language a business client reads when linking a card to a wallet — honest, plain, regulator-safe — approved line by line by the human before any of it enters a component.
**Surface(s)**: copy deck consumed by WALLET-006 (all three skins share identical copy — content never diverges per platform).
**Inputs**: DESIGN-000 research (tone of voice cues from Moneybase's public copy; competitor consent patterns), CLAUDE.md money-UX rules, B2B framing (company cardholder authority, "Aster Logistics Ltd").
**Output**: `03-content-creation/outputs/consent-explainer-deck.md` — the single copy source for the explainer + consent screens.

## Deck contents
- [ ] Explainer, ≤3 panels: (1) this card funds this wallet, (2) which currency is charged + when FX conversion applies, (3) what the client can change later — each ≤2 short sentences, no jargon
- [ ] Consent screen: summary of what is being agreed (bulleted, scannable), checkbox label, primary CTA label, decline button label
- [ ] Decline path copy: neutral, no guilt, states what happens to the draft and how to come back
- [ ] 2–3 variants per key line WITH rationale (tone, precision, legal risk) — human picks; rejected variants stay in the deck as evidence

## Hard rules
- [ ] Honest framing: no implied guarantees, no "instant/free/always", conditions visible not footnoted
- [ ] Every claim in the copy must be true of the mock service's actual behavior
- [ ] Reading level: plain business English; a non-native-speaker CFO understands it on first read
- [ ] Human approval recorded in the deck (approved ✅ / rejected ❌ per line) BEFORE WALLET-006 consumes it

## Out of scope
- Legal review simulation. Marketing copy. Error messages (CONTENT-002).
